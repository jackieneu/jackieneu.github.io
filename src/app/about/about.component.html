Hi, I’m Jackie. I’m a full-stack software engineer with a passion for building pragmatic software that provides an awesome user experience. I’ve been interested and involved in software development since 2009, when I was immersed in a programming environment during an undergraduate internship. I enjoyed it so much that I considered switching my major at the University of Wisconsin from industrial engineering to computer science, but I was so close to finishing that it seemed impractical; instead, I began working on (and eventually completed) a CS minor.

My first full-time position was in 2011 at SPX, a Fortune 500 company that makes power transformers. I completed rotations in continuous improvement and strategic sourcing but ultimately decided to focus on industrial engineering. In this role, I implemented projects for and advised the metal fabrication operations manager. I also wrote SAP formula codes for dynamic bills of material that determined manufacturing routes. This “light” coding made me really miss the deep challenges and problem solving that I experienced while programming, so I began working on a full computer science degree during my nights and weekends. Partway through this degree in 2014, I moved from SPX to FedEx, where I continued to gain valuable engineering, project management, and logistics experience. The tasks I enjoyed the most involved writing SQL queries to pull data for complex optimization projects that I designed and implemented.

I left FedEx in 2018 to pursue another long-term goal of mine: completing a “thru-hike” of the Pacific Crest Trail. This was an incredible experience that required large amounts of planning, dedication, and grit to complete.

Backpacking adventure (and CS degree) in the books, I made the switch into software engineering by joining a consulting company named MacGregor Partners. We made SaaS for package tracking and inventory management, which allowed me to leverage my logistics experience while working as a full-stack developer writing in Java, Spring, Hibernate, MySQL, and Angular. The 3-person team I worked on created database designs from scratch, used crazy 8s to create seamless user interface designs, performed multi-round code reviews, and verified success by writing unit, integration, and end-to-end tests. The startup environment here was exciting, but it confirmed my preference for larger companies. I moved to Northwestern Mutual in 2020, where I worked with an international team to review and maintain old enterprise code, update servers from HP-UX to UCP, perform risk engineering, write scripting for compliance feeds, and more. I also developed and maintained a compliance portal website and REST APIs using React, Python, Java, Spring, and SQL.

When I’m not working, I enjoy a variety of hobbies including rock climbing, “thru-hiking”, running, photography, and traveling.
